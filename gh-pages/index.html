Weather for your data

Connect a cloud account, pick a folder, and have a working backup in under a minute — while every rclone capability stays one deliberate tap away.

Get it on GitHub Read the docs

Open source · Android 8+ · Pre-release (0.1.0)

Virga backs up your phone to the cloud and keeps it there. You point it at a folder and an account; it copies your files up, watches for changes, and runs again when you've said it should. The hard part — talking to whichever storage you use — runs on the device through a bundled rclone engine, so you're never managing servers or pasting config by hand.

A virga is rain that falls but evaporates before it reaches the ground: weather you can watch, calm and legible, even mid-motion. That's the feeling we're after for your files. Backing up shouldn't be a spinner you stare at or a wall of jargon you decode. It's your data moving, and you can see exactly what's happening to it.

What you get

  • Set up in under a minute

    A guided first sync walks you through it: connect an account, pick a folder, done. No manual config, no rclone vocabulary before you're ready for it.

  • Your clouds, your way

    Sign in to Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox in a few taps. Need something else? Point Virga at S3, WebDAV, SFTP, and over 70 storage products through rclone.

  • One-way or two-way

    Upload, Download, or Two-way sync, whichever fits the folder. Mirror is opt-in, and it tells you exactly what it'll delete before it touches anything.

  • Runs on your terms

    Set a schedule, hold runs to Wi-Fi, wait for the charger, and cap upload speed separately on Wi-Fi and mobile data. Virga waits for the right moment instead of fighting your battery.

  • Private by design

    Your credentials are encrypted on the device, and rclone runs locally — nothing passes through anyone's server. There's no tracking, and crash reports stay off until you turn them on.

  • Power when you want it

    Filters, checksum verification, and a dry-run preview sit a tap away. The simple path stays simple; the depth shows up only when you reach for it.

Your files, nobody else's business

Virga has no account with us and no server in the middle. Here's what that means in practice.

  • Your cloud config is encrypted on the device using the Android Keystore.
  • The rclone engine binds to loopback only; it's never exposed to your network.
  • OAuth sign-in uses PKCE, and the tokens it returns stay on your phone.
  • The F-Droid build ships with no telemetry of any kind.
  • Crash reporting is opt-in, and it redacts file paths, remote names, and tokens.

How it works

  1. 1

    Connect a cloud account

    Sign in to Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox — or add another provider through rclone. Your credentials are encrypted and stay on the phone.

  2. 2

    Pick a folder to back up

    Choose a Source on your phone and a Destination in the cloud, then say which way it should flow: Upload, Download, or Two-way sync.

  3. 3

    Virga keeps it in sync

    Set a schedule and the conditions you want, then leave it alone. Virga runs on your terms and shows you what moved on every Run.
